      Asynchronous Hexes

      As part of their nature, Hexes evaluate in their entirety in one instant. To postpone Hexes for later, Nature has devised quite the whimsical solution: a Cassette Tape that must be crafted and then "consumed". I may have up to six at any time. Each one holds a Hex I requested to delay until that Hex is ready to cast. After casting, the Cassette becomes free to hold other Hexes.

      Enqueue (list, number → number)

      • Renders
        • Palettes
          Your browser does not support visualizing patterns. Pattern code: qeqwqwqwqwqeqaweqqqqqwweeweweewqdwwewewwewweweww

          Enqueues a Hex to cast after that many twentieths of a second. Queues it into the first free Cassette or the current Cassette and pushes the index of that Cassette.


          The index number is used to distinguish Cassettes from each other. It ranges from 0 to one less than the number of Cassettes I have in my mind's collection. If Enqueue can not find a free Cassette, incurs the Parallel Processing mishap.

          Seems to hint that the world I live in has a disturbingly low temporal resolution.. best not to dwell on that.

          Ibra oniki ra. QUINIO QUINIO QUINIO QUINIO alef a ra.


          I can ponder my Cassettes by pressing Ponder Cassettes and cycle through them with the arrow keys. Queued Hexes are represented as inserted Cassettes with their label rendered to the side, and by pressing an Cassette, I can cancel the corresponding Hex.

          Dying also forcibly disqueues all of my Cassettes. I can rest easy that I will not trap myself in an endless death cycle.


          If a Cassette enqueues a near-identical copy of itself, a phenomenon emerges that ancient Hexcasters call a daemon: passive, constant Hexcasting. Study into this branch of Hexcasting seems limited due to the great danger of manipulating time: Cassette Hexes exclusively use the media from my inventory. If left unchecked, it can easily drain my media and then my life.
